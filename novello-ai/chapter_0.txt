 Introduction – The Rise of the 100x Human
The day intelligence became cheap was a momentous one, marking a shift in the fabric of society that would fundamentally change how we interact with each other and our surroundings. For centuries, we had relied on our limited cognitive abilities to navigate life, but now, we could turn to machines for help – machines capable of processing vast amounts of data faster than any human could dream of.

This breakthrough was not simply a matter of technological progress; it signified a shift in how humans thought about themselves and their place in the world. We suddenly realized that our limitations were no longer an immutable fact of existence, but rather something we had chosen to accept for centuries.
We began to see ourselves as creatures who could rise above the constraints of biology and harness the power of machines to amplify our potential. The implications of this realization would reverberate throughout society, from the economy and politics to art and culture. In a very real sense, we were entering a new era – an era where human intelligence was no longer the sole driver of progress.

The day intelligence became cheap would go down in history as one of humanity's most significant achievements. And yet, like all great leaps forward, it also posed challenges that needed to be addressed. We had to confront our own biases and preconceptions about what constituted true intelligence, as well as consider the ethical implications of giving machines so much power over our lives. These were questions we could not afford to ignore if we wanted to ensure a bright future for all.

The day intelligence became cheap was a momentous occasion that marked a shift in how humans saw themselves and their potential. It also brought with it new challenges and opportunities, ones we must embrace if we are to make the most of this exciting era.
